Lentil Stew

Lentil stew is a hearty and flavorful dish that has been enjoyed across various cultures. This wholesome recipe starts with tender lentils simmered with a medley of vegetables and aromatic spices. Lentils are a great source of protein and fiber, making this stew not only delicious but also nutritious. As the stew cooks, its rich aroma fills the kitchen, promising a satisfying meal ahead.

  • 1 cup dried lentils (green, brown, or red).
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped.
  • 2 carrots, diced.
  • 2 celery stalks, diced.
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced.
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil.
  • 1 can (400g) diced tomatoes.
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth.
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin.
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric.
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika.
  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish.
  1. In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Saut√© until the vegetables become tender and the onion turns translucent.
  2. Add the minced garlic to the pot and cook for another minute, stirring frequently to avoid burning.
  3. Rinse the dried lentils thoroughly under cold water, then add them to the pot.
  4. Pour in the diced tomatoes and vegetable or chicken broth, stirring everything together.
  5. Season the stew with ground cumin, ground coriander, ground turmeric, paprika, salt, and pepper. Give it a good stir to distribute the spices evenly.
  6. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the lentils are tender and fully cooked.
  7. Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  8. Serve the warm lentil stew in bowls and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro for added freshness.
  9. Enjoy this comforting lentil stew with a slice of crusty bread or as a main dish on its own.
