Thieboudienne

The Thieboudienne is a delightful dish originating from Southeast Asian cuisine that has gained popularity worldwide. This delectable recipe starts with the selection of tender strips of meat, commonly chicken, marinated in a flavorful mixture of spices that include ginger, lemongrass, garlic, and soy sauce. After marinating for an appropriate time, the meat is threaded onto skewers and grilled to perfection. The enticing aroma of the cooking meat fills the air, tantalizing the senses.

  • Tender strips of chicken.
  • Ground ginger.
  • Lemongrass.
  • Garlic.
  • Soy sauce.
  • Rice.
  • Fresh vegetables (cabbage, carrots, beansprouts, etc.).
  • Cucumber.
  • Green onions.
  • Soy sauce for dipping sauce.
  • Skewers or bamboo sticks for threading the meat.
  • Oil for grilling.
  1. In a container, combine the chicken strips with ground ginger, chopped lemongrass, minced garlic, and soy sauce. Make sure the meat is evenly coated with the marinade and let it sit for at least 30 minutes to infuse the flavors.
  2. While the chicken marinates, prepare the dipping sauce. Mix soy sauce with some chopped green onions for added flavor.
  3. Preheat the grill or barbecue to medium-high heat. Thread the marinated chicken strips onto skewers or bamboo sticks.
  4. Grill the Thieboudienne on the grill for approximately 5-7 minutes on each side, or until the chicken is fully cooked and slightly charred.
  5. While the Thieboudienne cooks, cook the rice and prepare the fresh vegetables and cucumber for serving.
  6. To serve, place the grilled chicken on a plate, accompanied by rice and fresh vegetables. Garnish with sliced cucumber and serve with the soy sauce dipping sauce.
  7. Enjoy this delightful homemade Thieboudienne with your loved ones!
